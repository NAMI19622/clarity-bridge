# README Design Contract

## readmeArchetype
Translator's fidelity codex and before-and-after annotated study. The README
reads like a working translator's notebook on preserving meaning: each major
move is framed as an original-versus-simplified pairing with meaning-preservation
notes in the margin. This differs from persona-seal's consent charter (preamble
plus numbered Articles and lettered Annexes) and from the banned default
skeleton.

## readmeNarrativeVoice
The voice of a careful translator annotating their own work. Plain, exacting,
attentive to where meaning slips. It compares an original phrasing to a
simplified phrasing and explains what survived, what was at risk, and what the
gate would say. No marketing hype, no badge rows.

## readmeSectionPattern
Frontispiece (what the codex preserves and why meaning is the unit) ->
Reading 1: The Two Texts (the Concept Kernel and the Audience Explanation, shown
as original vs simplified) ->
Reading 2: Why Meaning Cannot Be Hashed (why documents and hashes cannot verify
a simplification, why fidelity is semantic, why GenLayer is essential) ->
Reading 3: The Six Verdicts (the gate outcomes and the consensus question) ->
Reading 4: The Margin Notes (the eight validators, each as an annotation, and
what it rejects) ->
Reading 5: What the Code Decides (deterministic guards before AI, deterministic
backstops after consensus, the state model and what each write mutates) ->
Reading 6: The Observatory (frontend architecture, visual and motion system) ->
Reading 7: Working Copy (local setup, testing) ->
Colophon (on-chain coordinates, deployment notes, limitations).

This reading-and-colophon order is not used by persona-seal and avoids the
banned default skeleton.

## readmeMotif
Annotated translation. Recurring "Original" and "Simplified" pairings with a
"Margin note" that records the meaning-preservation judgment. Key terms (Concept
Kernel, Essential Claim, Required Caveat, Forbidden Overclaim, Audience Lens,
Explanation Draft, Fidelity Gate, Clarity Certificate) are defined once in
Reading 1 and capitalized throughout.

## requiredFactsPlacement
- Project identity and one-sentence description: Frontispiece.
- What ClarityBridge is: Frontispiece and Reading 1.
- Why explanation fidelity is semantic: Reading 2.
- Why GenLayer is essential: Reading 2.
- Why documents and hashes cannot verify simplified meaning: Reading 2.
- The six verdicts and the consensus question: Reading 3.
- Validator substance and what each rejects: Reading 4.
- Deterministic guards before AI: Reading 5.
- Deterministic backstops after consensus: Reading 5.
- Contract state model: Reading 5.
- Write methods and what they mutate: Reading 5.
- View methods: Reading 5 and Colophon.
- Frontend architecture and visual/motion system: Reading 6.
- Local setup: Reading 7.
- Testing instructions: Reading 7.
- Live contract address and live frontend URL (pending deployment): Colophon.
- Deployment notes: Colophon.
- Known limitations: Colophon.

## bannedReadmePatterns
- The banned default heading skeleton (What it is / Why GenLayer is essential /
  Contract mechanic / ... / Known limitations).
- The persona-seal consent-charter shape (preamble plus Articles and Annexes).
- Opening with a marketing paragraph or a badge row.
- Emojis and em dashes.

## proofOfDifference
Versus persona-seal, whose README is a consent charter of numbered Articles and
lettered Annexes in a legal voice, ClarityBridge's README is a translator's
fidelity codex of numbered Readings with recurring Original-versus-Simplified
pairings and margin notes, in a translator's annotating voice. The document
metaphor, section order, and voice are all distinct.
